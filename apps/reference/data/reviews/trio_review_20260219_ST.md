## Observations and Lessons

### 1. Banker hit rate was excellent (81.8%)
The 1st-ranked horse placed top 3 in 9 of 11 races. Only two failures:
- **R3**: Mode C wide-open race — banker had only 18.9% Adj Win%, weakest banker of the day
- **R4**: Heavy favourite bust — #6 at 2.8 odds with 39.2% Adj Win% failed completely

### 2. Two recoverable misses from rule violations
- **R7**: #11 JUST FOLLOW ME hard-excluded for 16-day-old injury despite 9.2 odds. This miss directly led to Rule 2 (no hard exclusion if odds ≤ 15).
- **R9**: #14 ENTHRALLED (Adj Place% 30.8%) was only a reserve despite exceeding the 20% mandatory threshold. A wider Mode C pool (7 horses) would have captured this.

### 3. R1 #12 HE WAS ME — another mandatory rule violation
#12 had Adj Place% 24.5% (>= 20%) but was excluded from the pool. It placed 3rd at 18 odds. Even though the pool already missed for other reasons, this violation was noted and contributed to formalizing the mandatory inclusion rule.

### 4. Mode C races are the weakest
Both Mode C races (R3, R9) missed. In R3, the banker failed entirely. In R9, the banker was fine but a mandatory-threshold horse was excluded. Consider: Mode C races may not be worth playing Trio unless the pool is widened to 7+ horses with strict mandatory inclusion.

### 5. R8 was the star hit — $436 return on $100 cost
#14 JUBILANT WINNER came 1st at 6.5 odds, #1 HAPPY INDEX (banker) came 2nd, #9 THUNDER KIT came 3rd. All three were in the pool. Net +$336 from a single race.

### 6. R6 was the highest-ROI hit — $210 return on $60 cost
Small pool (5 horses, 6 combos with banker) but all three placers (#10, #11, #9) were in the pool. Net +$150 with only $60 at risk.
