## Section 8: Learnings

### What Worked

- **Rule 8 (Adj Place% ≥ 20% mandatory inclusion) delivered two unique hits.** R1 captured #1 JOYFUL PROSPERITY (Adj Place% 25.6%, won at 9.4x) and R6 captured #5 MOTOR (Adj Place% 22.1%, won at 15x). Neither horse would have been in a Mode B (6-horse) pool without this rule. Combined value: +$452 net from these two races alone.
- **+Excuses flag produced winners consistently.** Of the 9 race winners, 7 carried a +excuses flag from SCMP TIR data (R1#1, R2#3, R4#6, R5#1, R6#5, R8#7, R9#2). The signal identified bounce-back horses who had legitimate reasons for prior poor runs.
- **Purton jockey boost was highly reliable.** 6/7 mounts placed top 3 (85.7%). The +7% boost on MC top-4 runners correctly identified him as the key rider to anchor banker picks around.
- **Pool coverage at 7/9 (77.8%) is the best session yet.** Only 2 of 9 races had a pool gap (R3 #1 at 31x, R4 #10 at 16x — both deep longshots). The model's frame-building logic is sound.

### What Didn't Work

- **Dominant-classification banker reliability was poor (1/3 top 3).** R3 (#6 Bits Superstar, Adj 37.0%) and R8 (#8 California Moxie, Adj 50.0%) both failed despite being tagged Dominant. Only R7 (#2 Giant Ballon, Adj 50.0%) delivered. The Dominant label may create overconfidence in a single horse.
- **R2 over-investment: 28 combos ($280) on a 9-horse expanded pool.** The widest pool of the session generated the largest loss. When the pool expands beyond 7, staking cost escalates sharply (C(8,2)=28). A hard cap at 7 legs (21 combos) should be considered.
- **MC overrated #8 CALIFORNIA MOXIE in R8 (45.5% Win).** The horse carried strong prior Valley 1650m form, but the MC's extreme dominance rating (79% Place) proved inaccurate. Class 3 depth may have been undermodelled.
- **Missing R4 #10 EXCELLENT BOY was unavoidable but costly.** The $2,049 Trio dividend was the meeting's largest payout. No model signal (MC 1.6%, Adj Place% 8.2%, odds 24) would have included #10 — but the miss still hurts the session P&L narrative.

### Strategy Adjustments

- [ ] **Cap expanded pools at 7 legs maximum** — R2's 28-combo spend ($280) is disproportionate to expected returns on a Competitive race. When Rule 8 expands beyond 7, consider dropping the weakest legs to stay at C(6,2)=15 or C(7,2)=21.
- [ ] **Review Dominant-frame banker confidence**: when Adj Win% ≥ 35%, track whether a 雙膽拖 (dual banker) with #1 and #2 would have improved outcomes vs single banker. R3 and R8 suggest the answer is yes.
- [ ] **Weight +excuses flags more heavily in banker selection**: 7 of 9 winners carried +excuses. Consider a +excuses qualifier for banker selection (e.g., +excuses overrides MC #1 if #1 lacks excuses and #2 has them).
- [ ] **Track late SP drift as a negative banker signal**: R1 banker #2 drifted 6.4→8.6, R5 banker #1 drifted 4.9→7.2. Both placed. But R2 banker #1 was pushed 4.7→2.8 (supported) and failed. Over-support in the market didn't predict success.
