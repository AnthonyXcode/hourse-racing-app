## Observations and Lessons

### 1. Banker structure destroyed $11,946 in value
R1 and R4 both had **perfect pool selection** (all 3 finishers in pool) but the banker failed. Full pool would have captured $1,167 + $10,779 = $11,946. This is the single largest strategic error.

### 2. Capping to top 5 loses coverage, not gains it
The model's pool selection was correct in R1 (7-horse pool covered all finishers) and R4 (6-horse pool covered all finishers). Reducing to 5 drops #11 in R1 (finished 2nd) and #2 in R4 (finished 2nd). **The problem was never pool size — it was the banker.**

### 3. Rule 2 violations cost 3 potential hits
R6 (#6 at 12 odds), R8 (#10 at 14 odds), and R9 (#12 at 13 odds) all had horses with SCMP Win ≤ 15 excluded from the pool that placed top 3. Two of these (R6, R9) also had the banker hold — adding those horses would have yielded $1,775 + $1,953 = $3,728.

### 4. Happy Valley banker rate far below Sha Tin
- **19-Feb ST**: 9/11 = 81.8%
- **22-Feb ST**: 5/10 = 50.0%
- **25-Feb HV**: 4/9 = **44.4%**
HV's tight track produces more upsets. The banker strategy should be venue-adjusted.

### 5. The optimal strategy for tonight: full pool, no banker
Full pool C(P,3) at $2,300 total stake would have returned $12,076 (ROI +425%). Even with 5 misses costing $1,300, the 4 hits more than compensated. The R4 jackpot ($10,779) alone covered the entire meeting's stake 4.7 times over.
