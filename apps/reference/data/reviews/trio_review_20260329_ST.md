## Section 8: Learnings

### What worked

- **雙膽拖 when both anchors land (R11)** produced the best ROI of the session ($179 on $40).
- **Single banker + six legs (R10)** hit a **$120** Trio with straightforward coverage — proof the base structure still works when the banker stays in the frame.
- **Pool coverage was deep** — eight races had all three horses inside the written pool; misses were usually **one horse** or **banker placement**, not wholesale model blindness.

### What didn’t work

- **Pattern A twice (R1, R7)** — correct six horses in the pool, wrong horse wearing the banker star.
- **Pattern B on R6** — the costliest miss of the season-to-date single-meeting type ($2,081) for one missing leg (**#7**).
- **R5 #6** — sub-10 win odds but not in the leg list; **market-visible** horses below the cut need a hard rule.
- **R9 雙膽** — **#6** as banker when **#7** was the co-anchor created a structural fail despite full pool coverage.

### Strategy adjustments

- [ ] **Short-odds mandatory leg:** If any runner has **Win SP &lt; 10**, force into the leg set unless explicitly PASS (evidence: R5 **#6**).
- [ ] **R6-style gap:** After dual bankers are set, add **one** “next Adj Place%” leg when the field is **Class 2/3** and combo count ≤3 (evidence: **#7** gap).
- [ ] **Pattern A review:** When all three placers are in legs pre-race (simulated), consider **banker rotation** to MC #2 or market #1 (evidence: R1, R7).
- [ ] **雙膽 banker parity:** If co-bankers split MC and market, require **both** to have **Adj Place%** above threshold (evidence: R9 **#6**).
- [ ] **Re-read R8 longshot tail:** Winner **#1** locked; extend one **longshot leg** when favourite MC Win% &gt; 35% (evidence: **#10** at ~31x).
