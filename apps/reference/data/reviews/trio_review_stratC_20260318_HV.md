## Why 4 Hits with “Place%>20% OR odds<10”?

Adding **odds < 10** as a leg criterion (Strategy B) improved results vs legs = Place%>20% only (Strategy C):

| Race | Place%>20% only | + odds<10 (Strategy B) |
|------|------------------|-------------------------|
| R4 | Miss (#12 16.7% not in pool) | **Hit** — #12 odds 4 < 10 |
| R8 | Miss (#11 15.7% not in pool) | **Hit** — #11 odds 7.6 < 10 |
| R9 | Miss (#12 18.0% not in pool) | **Hit** — #12 odds 7.1 < 10 |

**Summary**: 3 of the 4 hits (R4, R8, R9) came from horses that qualified only via **odds < 10**. The market (short odds) captured horses that MC underrated by Place%. Stake rose ($1,270 vs $990) but returns rose more ($1,094 vs $116), so P&L improved from -$874 to -$176 and ROI from -88.3% to -13.9%.

## Lessons

1. **“Place%>20% OR odds<10” beats “Place%>20% only”**: On this day, three extra hits (R4, R8, R9) came from short-priced horses that MC had below 20% Place%. The market added useful legs.
2. **Banker failure is still fatal** (R2, R7): No change; when the banker misses the frame, the bet loses.
3. **Stake increases are acceptable if hit rate rises**: Strategy B staked $280 more but returned $978 more, improving P&L by $698.
4. **Keep banker = MC #1**: Same as Strategy C; no need to change banker rule on this evidence.
