## Lessons

1. **"Place%>20% OR odds<10" caught the R10 WINNING OVATION payoff**: MC gave 0.0%/0.1% but odds 4.6 forced inclusion — and it won. The odds<10 rule is essential for bridging MC blind spots on zero-form horses.
2. **Banker failure remains the #1 loss driver**: 3/10 races lost to banker failure (R1, R2, R4). All were C4 races with MC #1 at 18–26% Win — the model's banker selection is less reliable in lower-class competitive fields.
3. **Thin-tail races produce thin legs**: R6 had only 3 legs because the co-dominant #4/#3 duo absorbed 80% of win probability, leaving no other horse with Place%>20% or odds<10. The strategy correctly spent only $30, but still missed.
4. **Strategy B and Strategy A converged on the same 4 hits**: The simpler MC-only approach matched the full pipeline's hit set, though A saved $90 via 雙膽拖 staking efficiency. Both approaches are structurally sound on Sha Tin form.
5. **Cross-meeting: 8/19 hits (42.1%) with -18.7% ROI**: The hit rate is solid but Trio dividends have been too small (avg $257 per hit) relative to stake sizes. Need higher-dividend hits (= value outsiders in the frame) to turn profitable.
