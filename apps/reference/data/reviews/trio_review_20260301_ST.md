## Miss Classification (6 races)

### Pattern A: Banker Fail — All 3 Placers Already in Legs (R5, R6) — 2 misses

The most frustrating pattern. In both races, all three actual top-3 finishers were in our leg pool, but the banker didn't make top 3. The 膽拖 structure killed us.

- **R5**: Banker #3 ISLAND BUDDY (12 odds, Adj Win% 27.4%) missed top 3. Result: #6→#4→#12 — all legs.
  - #3 had back-to-back C&D wins but couldn't sustain the streak.
  - If bet as 7-horse any-combo instead of 膽拖, this hits.
  - **Missed Trio: $210**

- **R6**: Banker #6 PERFECTDAY (McDonald, Adj Win% 24.8%) missed top 3. Result: #3→#2→#4 — all legs.
  - #3 GALACTIC VOYAGE (the original MC #1 before the re-run) won at $3.8.

**Root cause**: Over-reliance on single banker in competitive races where the top 2-3 horses are closely matched.

**Potential fix**: For Mode B races where the top 2 horses are within 5% Adj Win%, consider 雙膽拖 (dual banker) to protect against the #1 pick failing. This would increase combos but improve resilience.

## Learnings

### What Worked
1. **Contrarian bankers delivered**: R1 (#9 at 14), R10 (#1 at 11) — MC divergence was profitable. The model's ability to identify undervalued horses is its biggest strength.
2. **Dual-banker structure (R7)**: The 雙膽拖 format perfectly suited the G1 6-runner field. Both bankers placed, and the 88-1 surprise was caught as a leg. Only 4 combos for $40 — exceptional efficiency.
3. **Rule 2 justified (R11)**: #4 MUGEN was included despite poor form because Purton rode and odds were ≤15. He finished 3rd. Rule 2 works.
4. **Mode B+ (expanded pool) paid off**: R1, R10, R11 all used 7-horse pools and hit. The broader net is worth the extra combos.
5. **Hit rate 45.5%**: Dramatically improved from the 14.3% (1/7) on 19-Feb. The unified pool, mandatory inclusion rules, and jockey boosts all contributed.

### What Didn't Work
1. **Single banker fragility**: 5 of 6 misses had banker failure. In competitive races (R5, R6), the gap between banker and #2 pick was narrow (<5% Adj Win%) — a dual banker would have been safer.
2. **Mode A pool too narrow (R3)**: The 5-horse pool couldn't absorb a 42-1 upset. Dominant classification should still allow at least 6 horses.
3. **Re-run shifted wrong (R6)**: The odds-change re-run moved the banker from #3 GALACTIC VOYAGE to #6 PERFECTDAY. #3 won. The re-run hurt rather than helped — chasing odds movement was counterproductive.
4. **Prestigious race volatility (R9)**: Classic Cup was too unpredictable for a banker-based approach. Big-race 4YO events may warrant a PASS or Mode C (wide-open) treatment.

### Strategy Adjustments
- [ ] **Dual banker threshold**: If top 2 horses are within 5% Adj Win% in Mode B, auto-upgrade to 雙膽拖. Evidence: R5 (27.4% vs 21.2%), R6 (24.8% vs ~22%).
- [ ] **Mode A minimum 6 horses**: Expand Dominant pool from 5 to 6 to capture tail risk. Evidence: R3 (#14 at 42-1 placed 2nd).
- [ ] **Caution on odds-change re-runs**: Late odds shifts should only trigger a re-run if the change is >30%. Minor shifts (<20%) should not alter the banker.
- [ ] **Prestige race downgrade**: Group/Terms races with large fields (14+ runners) should be classified one tier lower (e.g., Competitive → Wide Open) to account for higher volatility. Evidence: R9 Hong Kong Classic Cup.
