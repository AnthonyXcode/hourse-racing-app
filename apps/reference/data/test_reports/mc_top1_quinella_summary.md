# MC Top 1 Quinella Strategy — Cross-Meeting Summary

## Strategy

- **Selection**: Bet the **#1 Quinella pair** from MC simulation (highest MC probability)
- **Bet**: Quinella ($10) on that single pair each race
- **Stake per race**: $10
- **Win condition**: The two horses finish 1st and 2nd in any order → collect Quinella dividend (per $10 unit)

The highest-probability picks (≥20%) are **0/5** — R4 (28.2%), R6 (29.4%), R6 (43.8%), R9 (18.1%), R11 (18.5%). The model's "certainties" fail to convert because the market already prices them tightly, and any disruption (an outsider sneaking into 2nd) kills the quinella.

### 22 Mar wipeout (0/10)

The worst meeting shows how volatile this strategy is. 7 of 10 misses had one horse correct, but the second horse always differed. Notable:
- **R6**: MC gave 3-4 at **43.8%** probability — the highest confidence pick in the entire dataset — but #1 (14x) beat #3 to 2nd. The market Quinella for 1-4 likely paid well, but the model's pair was wrong.
- **R9**: 5-9 at 18.1% — the 2nd ranked pair (2-9) actually hit. Staking Top 2 would have captured $32.50.

### Break-even analysis

- Average dividend per hit: **$102.67**
- At $10/race, need 1 hit per 10.3 races to break even
- Actual rate: 3 hits in 30 races = 1 per 10 races → **barely above break-even**
- The strategy's entire profit ($8) depends on the two overlay hits from 25 Mar and 29 Mar
