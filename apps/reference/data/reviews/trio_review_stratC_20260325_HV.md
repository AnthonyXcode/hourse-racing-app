## Lessons

1. **Odds<10 added no value this session**: zero extra hits from the criterion, but $330 extra staking. The 22 Mar R10 WINNING OVATION payoff ($385) remains the only odds<10-driven hit in 3 meetings. The rule works in edge cases but inflates costs on most nights.
2. **Place%>20% only would have been profitable (+$86)**: the purest MC-Place threshold produced leaner pools and the same 4 hits. Consider whether odds<10 should be a conditional add (e.g., only when banker MC Win% ≥ 30%) rather than blanket.
3. **Pool gap on #5 MOTOR (R6) highlights SCMP's value**: MC Place% 17.0% and odds 12 — neither threshold captured the eventual winner. Strategy A's excuses boost was the difference. Strategy B structurally cannot access this signal.
4. **R5 hit with negative P&L ($187 < $210)**: when the frame is all chalk (top-3 MC contenders), expanded pools produce expensive tickets against low-dividend outcomes. A staking cap at 15 combos ($150) would have turned this into +$37.
5. **Banker failure at 3/9 (33.3%) is consistent**: across 3 meetings, 8/28 bankers failed (28.6%). The MC #1 banker is reliable but not immune to upsets, especially in Competitive races.
