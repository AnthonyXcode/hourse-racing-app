## Lessons

1. **Worst banker session in 5 meetings**: 4/9 (44.4%) banker placed — down from 63.6%–77.8% in prior meetings. Average MC Place% of failed bankers was **88.9%** — the model was extremely confident and extremely wrong. The AWT surface at Sha Tin produced outsized upsets.
2. **Odds<10 enabled R4 hit but at a net loss**: The rule's flagship moment — capturing #1 PI LEGEND ($3.6 fav, MC 0%) to complete the frame. But the $77 Trio dividend on a $150 stake = -$73. The rule works structurally but needs higher-paying results to be profitable.
3. **Pattern A in R6 was the costliest miss**: All 3 placers in the leg pool (including odds<10 adds #1 and #6), banker #3 at MC 73.1% Win finished 5th. $1,249 Trio dividend was fully covered structurally but unrecoverable due to banker failure. This is the **recurring weakness** of the 1-banker system.
4. **R7 odds<10 adds placed 1st and 2nd**: #1 IGOR STRAVINSKY ($6.3, MC 0.7%) won and #11 CONRAD THE GREAT ($7.1, MC 2.1%) placed 2nd — both captured by odds<10. But banker #5 finished 4th and #4 FUN N FUN TOGETHER (not in any pool) placed 3rd. Agonising near-miss.
5. **R2 was the unfixable miss**: #9 VERBIER at 81-to-1 placed 3rd. No sensible rule would include a horse with 0.5% MC Place% and 42x odds. $6,792 Trio dividend — the biggest of the night — was structurally impossible to capture.
6. **AWT surface effect**: All 9 races on the all-weather track. The MC's dominant assessments (65%+ Win% in R1, R3, R6) repeatedly failed. The AWT appears to compress class differentials more than turf, creating more upset-prone outcomes.
7. **Cumulative drawdown accelerating**: -$2,847 through 48 races (ROI -41.1%), down from -$1,887/-33.6% after Meeting 4. Two consecutive Sha Tin meetings (29 Mar, 1 Apr) produced combined -$2,131 on $2,770 staked.
