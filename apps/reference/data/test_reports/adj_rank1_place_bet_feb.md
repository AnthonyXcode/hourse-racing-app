## Comparison: Adj Win% #1 (Feb) vs MC Win% #1 (Mar)

| Metric | Adj Win% #1 (Feb meetings) | MC Win% #1 (Mar meetings) |
|--------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| Ranking | Adj Win% (MC + jockey + SCMP) | Raw MC Win% (no adjustments) |
| Meetings | 19 Feb, 22 Feb, 25 Feb | 18 Mar, 22 Mar, 25 Mar |
| Races | 30 | 28 |
| Placed | **18/30 (60.0%)** | **21/28 (75.0%)** |
| Staked | $300 | $280 |
| Returned | $265.2 | $386.7 |
| **P&L** | **-$34.8** | **+$106.7** |
| **ROI** | **-11.6%** | **+38.1%** |
| Avg Place Div (when placed) | $14.7 | $18.4 |

### Key Differences

1. **Place rate gap: 60% vs 75%.** The raw MC #1 placed 15 percentage points more often. This is counterintuitive — the "smarter" adjusted ranking underperforms the simpler raw ranking.

2. **Avg Place dividend: $14.7 vs $18.4.** When the Adj Win% #1 places, it pays less. This is because SCMP adjustments tend to reinforce market favourites (short odds → low Place dividends). The raw MC #1 sometimes disagrees with the market, producing higher-dividend places.

3. **Feb had a tougher schedule.** The 25 Feb HV meeting was particularly brutal (4/9 Place rate, 5 banker busts including a 2.2 favourite). Whether the underperformance is structural or variance-driven requires more data.

4. **SCMP adjustments may be adding noise, not signal.** The adjustments shift the ranking toward market-aligned picks, but the market is already priced into odds. When the model agrees with the market, the dividend is small; when it's wrong, you lose. Raw MC picks that disagree with the market capture more value when they're right.
