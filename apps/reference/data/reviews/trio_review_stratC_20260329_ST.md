## Lessons

1. **Worst single-meeting loss in 4 sessions**: -$1,171 (ROI -79.7%) despite 7/11 bankers placing. The problem was not banker reliability — it was **pool gaps** on 5 of 7 banker-placed races (R2, R4, R5, R6, R8).
2. **Pattern B dominated (5/11 misses)**: Banker placed correctly but one required horse was outside the pool. The gap horses: #14 (25x), #7 (17x), #6 (10x), #7 (11x), #10 (31x). All but R5's #6 were double-digit longshots — structurally difficult to capture without inflating pools.
3. **Pattern A repeated (4/11)**: Full pool coverage with banker failure. R1 and R7 especially frustrating — every placed horse was a leg, but the designated banker missed.
4. **R6 $2,081 was the biggest single-race missed dividend in 4 meetings**: Banker #3 placed 3rd, leg #8 won, but #7 FRANCIS MEYNELL (8.1%, 12) was the gap. Neither threshold captured a horse with 8.1% MC Place% and 12x odds.
5. **Odds<10 added $350 cost, zero hits across 11 races**: Continues the trend from Meetings 1-3. Cumulative odds<10 hit contribution remains marginal at best.
6. **R10 hit with negative P&L (-$30)**: 15-combo pool on a chalk frame. The odds<10 add #7 expanded pool from 10cb to 15cb for no benefit — Place%>20% only would have yielded +$20.
7. **R11 was the session's sole profitable hit (+$119)**: Tight 4-leg pool (6 combos) on a well-framed race. MC correctly ranked all three placers in the top 3 by Win%.
