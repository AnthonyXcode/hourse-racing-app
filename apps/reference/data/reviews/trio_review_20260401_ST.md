## Section 8: Learnings

### What worked

- **R8 雙膽拖 precision:** The cheapest ticket ($30) delivered the only Strategy A hit ($263). MC's dominant assessment of Victory Sky (58% Win) was spot-on. The lesson: when the MC truly identifies a dominant horse at Class 3+ level, 雙膽拖 is the optimal structure.
- **+Excuses flag at 42.9% top-3 rate:** Horses flagged with excuses placed significantly better than baseline, confirming the SCMP adjustment is adding real value. Notable winners: #12 LESLIE (R3), #5 HAPPY UNIVERSE (R5), #4 LOCH TAY (R9).
- **Strategy B's win-odds addition rule caught R4:** The explicit rule forcing market favourites (Win odds <10, MC Place% ≤20%) into Strategy B pools paid off — #1 PI LEGEND ($2.4 fav, MC 0%) won, and Strategy B was the only strategy to cover this.
- **Pool depth for at least 2 placers:** 6/9 races had at least 2 of 3 result horses in the Strategy A pool — the model isn't blind, it's the banker selection and third-horse coverage that need work.

### What didn't work

- **Banker reliability collapsed:** Individual banker top-3 rate was **42.9%** (6/14) — far below the ~63.6% from the previous meeting. Four of five 雙膽拖 races needed both bankers in the frame; only 2/5 succeeded.
- **MC overconfidence on "dominant" races:** 7 of 9 races were classified as "Dominant" (top Adj Win% ≥35%). Yet the MC #1 pick finished outside the top 3 in **5 of those 7** races. The AWT surface appears to create more upset-prone outcomes than the model captures.
- **Purton-anchored tickets failed early card:** R1 #3 (65% MC, 10th), R3 #4 (66% MC, 5th), R5 #2 (32% MC, 7th), R6 #3 (73% MC, 5th). The jockey boost inflated already-dominant MC picks, making the model even more overconfident.
- **0/3 placers in pool (R7):** A complete model blind spot. The MC's top 2 (#5 at 39%, #2 at 29%) both missed the frame on a night where the all-weather surface levelled the field.
- **R1 ONLY U debacle:** The most-fancied horse across all 9 races (65% MC Win, Purton, 3.6 odds) finished last. No vet issues found. This single event destroyed the R1 ticket and shook confidence in the model's AWT sprint assessments.

### Strategy adjustments

- [ ] **AWT surface discount:** When the entire meeting is AWT (as 1 Apr was), apply a **−5%** discount to the top MC Win% horse if it exceeds 50%, reflecting the surface's upset tendency (evidence: R1, R3, R6 dominant picks all failed).
- [ ] **Mandatory market-favourite leg:** If any horse has **SP < 5** and is **not** in the Strategy A pool, force it in as an additional leg regardless of MC rating (evidence: R4 #1 at $2.4, R6 #1 at $5.9 — both placed).
- [ ] **雙膽拖 caution at Class 4/5:** Restrict 雙膽拖 to Class 3 or above. For Class 4/5 on AWT, default to **single banker** with wider legs (evidence: 雙膽 lost R5, R6, R7 at C4; hit R4 and R8 — but R4 also missed due to pool gap).
- [ ] **Banker rotation for Purton-boosted picks:** If Purton's jockey boost pushes a horse from MC #2 to Adj #1, consider keeping the raw MC #1 as the banker instead (evidence: R5 #5 was raw MC #1 but #2 became Adj #1 via Purton; #5 won while #2 flopped).
- [ ] **Early race entropy premium:** For R1–R3 on AWT in Class 5, widen the pool by one extra leg (Mode B minimum) even if classified Dominant (evidence: R1 and R3 both classified Dominant but produced upsets with $992 and $7,685 Trios).
